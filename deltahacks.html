The design of the sixth hackathon for change

As one of the largest hackathons in Canada, DeltaHacks brings together over 800 of the tech world’s most creative, enthusiastic and passionate students to McMaster University for a phenomenal weekend - 24 hours of hacking on all platforms to create hundreds of groundbreaking projects from scratch. Whatever their focus may be - healthcare, education, environment, accessibility - students strive to create a positive impact through their ideas and projects.

PRELUDE

Theme Generation

There are certain aspects of the design process that often go unnoticed. It is the little steps that come together to help create the end product that everyone sees. We started from scratch, referring to the user-centered design process because we knew we were going to create designs for potentially over 1600 users. UX and UI are essential facets to the process and although the terms seem almost identical, they have their differences and are essential to the design process. In a nutshell, UX focuses on the user and their journey through the product and UI is focused on how a product’s surfaces look and function. We wanted to create products that would satisfy the end user’s needs by creating a simple theme while staying true to our brand. We spent nights deciding on a consistent theme for DeltaHacks VI. Unconventionally, we didn’t refer to Behance or Dribble for inspiration, rather tried to allow our imagination to take over and design whatever came to our mind. We eventually decided to stray slightly away from the space theme, and stick to a more general simplistic and minimalistic theme.
