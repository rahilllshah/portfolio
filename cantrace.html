OVERVIEW

A healthcare driven contact tracing app to mitigate the spread of COVID-19

I was reached out to in the early stages of the COVID-19 pandemic to join a team working on an application to mitigate the spread of the virus. Through many sleepless nights and slurps of coffee, we got the chance to present our product to the provincial and federal government of Canada which was an intriguing experience!


Duration

16 weeks


Team

Role

Product Designer

BACKGROUND

The deadly spread of COVID-19

COVID-19 is a respiratory virus and spreads primarily via droplets, including through coughing and sneezing. Furthermore, the virus can also be transmitted prior to symptom onset or from entirely asymptomatic cases which makes tracing contacts more difficult. As we begin to relax quarantine restrictions, we need to find a way to more efficiently monitor transmission to prevent additional waves of virus transmission.

BACKGROUND

Why we need to reduce transmission

At present, current isolation and quarantine measures in place are in the hope of reducing occurrence of novel cases and “flatten the curve.” With limited ICU and medical care capacity, the goal is to limit the healthcare burden associated with surges in cases. Contact tracing as an integral practice that the healthcare system has currently employed to ensure isolation of at-risk contacts and curb further spread.

BACKGROUND

What is contact tracing?

Contact tracing works by having a health care staff identify an infected person and then manually trace back that patient’s steps in the past two weeks to find out where they may have gotten the infection from and who else they might have exposed to the infection. We have a platform for public health to use to expedite the process of contact tracing and help conquer COVID-19!

PROBLEM

Current contact tracing apps fall short

Apps focus primarily on notifying users if they have had an exposure risk

PROBLEM

What is missing from current apps?

SOLUTION

Our solution

Without healthcare integration, apps do little to support contact tracing efforts by public health. CanTrace bridges technology with healthcare to create an effective tool against the infection, expedite recovery, and preserve civil liberties for the common good of all Canadians.

FINAL DESIGN

The simplest and seamless onboarding process out there

The idea of getting your users familiar with the app and its features is extremely important. This process allows us to strategically communicate with our users and facilitate a positive user experience right from the get-go.

You're also stratified to your local public health unit

To enable accurate healthcare integration while maximizing privacy and security - the user's province and postal code is solicited to assign them a unit closest to them. No personal information is saved.

FINAL DESIGN

If you've tested positive for COVID-19

Just enter the verification code texted to you by your public health unit. The app will then provide you with the resources and material to help guide you through your diagnosis.

FINAL DESIGN

If you came in close contact with someone who tested positive for COVID-19

Risk levels may vary on the situation. Therefore, we have implemented a contact tracing triage self-assesment tool to assess your risk; and provide advice accordingly.

LEARNINGS & REFELCTIONS

The ups and downs of building an app for the country

When you're building a product that can potentially be used by 35 million people. The way your mind thinks changes a little and you get that extra motivation to complete the product with perfection and work with your colleagues in going through the entire design process thoroughly and effectively. By the time we presented our product to the government, they had already started developing their own app with Shopify and Blackberry and so we didn't have much of a chance. However, we had ideas and features that were not and are still not implemented into the COVID Alert app released by the government. The biggest learning I would take from this whole experience is to think swiftly, you never know when the opportunity may close.
